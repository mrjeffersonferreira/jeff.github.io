Machado de Assis

The biggest name in Brazilian literature.

Here's a time line of Machado de Assis's life:

  • 1839 - Joaquim Maria Machado de Assis is born in Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1855 - Publish in the magazine Marmota Fluminense his first literary work, the poem "Ela".
  • 1858/59 - He collaborates in O Paraíba, from Petrópolis, and in the magazine O Espelho, mainly with verses.
  • 1861 - Publish Queda que as Mulheres Têm para o Tolos; Desencantos; Hoje Avental, Amanhã Luva, theater.
  • 1863 - Publish O Caminho da Porta and O Protocolo, theater, and at the end of that year the first performance of his new comedy is made Quase Ministor .
  • 1864 - Debut in poetry with the publication of the work Crisálidas.
  • 1865 - The work Deuses de Casaca is presented for the first time, a play theater in verse.
  • 1869 - He marries Carolina Augusta Xavier de Novais.
  • 1870 - Publish Falenas, poems, and Contos Fluminenses, collection of short stories.
  • 1872 - He made his debut in romance with the publication of Ressurreição.
  • 1873 - Publish Histórias da Meia-Noite, short stories. He is appointed first officer of the State Secretariat of the Ministry of Agriculture, Commerce and Public Works.
  • 1874 - He published his second novel A Mão e a Luva.
  • 1875 - Publish Americanas, poems.
  • 1876 - Publish Helena, romance.
  • 1878 - Publish Iaiá Garcia, romance.
  • 1879 - He began to collaborate in "Revista Brasileira, with literary criticism and poems.
  • 1880 - He is appointed cabinet officer at the Secretariat of Agriculture. That same year he began, in chapters, the Posthumous Memoirs of Brás Cubas. The play, Tu, só Tu, Puro Amor is performed at the Imperial College, on the occasion of the festivities commemorating the III Centenary of Camões' death.
  • 1881 - Publish Memórias Póstumas de Brás Cubas, romance.
  • 1882 - Publish Papéis Avulsos, short stories.
  • 1883 - He effectively collaborates with the newspaper Gazeta de Notícias with its section "Balas de Estalo".
  • 1884 - Publish Histórias sem Data, short stories.
  • 1891 - Publish Quincas Borba, romance.
  • 1896 - Publish Várias Histórias, short stories.
  • 1897 - He is acclaimed president of the Brazilian Academy of Letters. He loses his position as general director of Transport, becoming merely an attaché.
  • 1899 - Publish Dom Casmurro, romance, and Páginas Recolhidas, short stories.
  • 1901 - Publish Poesias Completas.
  • 1904 - Publish Esaú e Jacó, romance. His wife Carolina passes away.
  • 1906 - Publish Relíquias de Casa Velha, collection of stories, also including the famous sonnet "A Carolina".
  • 1908 -He publishes his latest novel, Memorial de Aires. He passed away on September 29th.

Most countries have a writer like him: the bearded eminence whose face adorns postage stamps, and whose name dignifies avenues, and whose Complete Works sit, undisturbed, on grandparents’ bookshelves.

-- The New Yorker
